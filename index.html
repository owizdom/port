i grew up in nigeria, raised on books by physicists and historians. curiosity shaped most of my childhood. i spent years tinkering with things — building small robot cars and planes, taking apart hardware, trying to understand how systems worked.

when resources ran out, i drifted toward software. i taught myself to code on school computers, sometimes writing qbasic on paper because i didn't have a laptop to run them on. i moved through phases — robotics, cybersecurity, programming — learning whatever i could find online.

eventually i found my way into blockchain and open systems, and later into ai and ml research. both felt like the same thing to me: technology, economics, and big ideas about how the world could work. these days i sit somewhere between the two.

life since then hasn't been linear. i left my first university after a few months, moved abroad to study in kigali, lost jobs, slept on couches, and had long stretches where nothing felt stable. but those years taught me a lot about resilience and about building even when things are uncertain.

i haven't figured everything out yet. and honestly, i'm okay with that. more experiments, more work, more growth. let's see where it goes.
